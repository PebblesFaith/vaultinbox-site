What problem we’re solving

Certified mail and legacy delivery workflows are costly, slow, and vulnerable to loss and evidentiary gaps. Modern regulated communications require stronger identity assurance, clearer chain-of-custody, and defensible records.

Solution

Digital certified delivery with traceable delivery events, auditable logs, and evidence-oriented documentation patterns.

  • Evidence-oriented certificate generation
  • Verifiable delivery event capture
  • Structured audit trail and retention posture

Why it’s defensible

Auditability and access-control discipline are engineered into core workflows—not bolted on after the fact.

  • Control-category aligned design (least privilege, defense in depth)
  • Event integrity and traceability as product requirements
  • Evidence outputs designed for review and verification

Who it’s for

Organizations and individuals who send communications that must stand up to scrutiny.

  • Legal notices and formal correspondence
  • Compliance communications and policy delivery
  • Dispute documentation and recordkeeping

Evidence posture (what can be proven)

Delivery events

  • When an item was prepared and sent
  • Recipient access actions (where appropriate)
  • Timestamped event trail for review

Integrity

  • Audit trail designed to be reviewable
  • Evidence-oriented certificate outputs
  • Change awareness and accountability

Access control

  • Least-privilege authorization mindset
  • Defense-in-depth security controls
  • Selective disclosure for sensitive details

Investor FAQ

How is this different from email?

VaultInbox is designed around auditable delivery events and evidence-oriented documentation for high-trust communications, not general-purpose messaging.

How do you handle security disclosure?

Public pages describe principles and control categories. Security-sensitive implementation and evidentiary design details are shared selectively during diligence and partnership discussions.

What is the initial revenue model?

Usage-based (pay-per-delivery) with optional add-ons for certificate copies and enterprise features as the platform matures.

What does “audit-grade” mean here?

It means workflows are designed for traceability and review: event integrity, defensible records, and documentation patterns suitable for audit and evidentiary contexts.
