Development status

A concise view of current execution. This page is intended to communicate delivery maturity, engineering focus, and readiness for pilot engagement—without hype.

Now

Core certified digital delivery workflows are implemented and validated end-to-end: draft creation, tracking ID generation, receipt/price preview, payment-confirmed finalization, delivery state transitions, and evidence-oriented certificate output. The system is designed to replace physical proof-of-delivery workflows commonly handled via USPS Certified Mail and carrier document services.

Next

Reliability hardening and negative-case validation: authentication edge cases, replay resistance, rate limiting, session controls, and operational monitoring. This phase focuses on strengthening the audit narrative under stress conditions and preparing the platform for limited-scope pilots with real compliance senders.

Later

Expansion toward enterprise and institutional usage: bulk sending patterns, API-based integrations, policy-driven access controls, and formal compliance readiness (e.g., SOC 2–aligned control mapping once operational scale justifies certification).

Execution posture

VaultInbox is being developed as evidence infrastructure—not a general messaging tool. Product decisions prioritize controlled state transitions, defensible event timelines, and clarity under audit or dispute review. Market expansion across USPS-, UPS-, and FedEx-style proof workflows is being addressed deliberately, with reliability and integrity ahead of growth acceleration.
